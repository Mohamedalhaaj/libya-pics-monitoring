## 🔴 Politics & Governance

### Tripoli Government Signs Military Cooperation Deal with Niger (17 June)
The Government of National Unity (GNU) signed a military cooperation agreement with Niger, focusing on border security and joint training. Prime Minister Abdul Hamid Dbeibah held talks with Nigerien PM Ali Zeine in Tripoli.

### Protesters Shut Down IOM Office in Tripoli Over Migration Policies (16–17 June)
Protesters stormed and shut down the International Organization for Migration (IOM) office in Tripoli, denouncing the handling of illegal immigration and rejecting settlement of foreign nationals in Libya. The National Institution for Human Rights in Libya (NIHRL) condemned the closure.

### Libya Hosts Trilateral Security Meeting with Algeria & Tunisia (16 June)
Tripoli hosted the second session of the Libyan-Algerian-Tunisian working group on border security. Talks covered border protection, organised crime, counter-terrorism, smuggling networks, and irregular migration. Major General Mahmoud Saeed chaired the meeting.

### EU Reiterates Support for Libya on Migration and Border Security (15–16 June)
The European Union reaffirmed support for Libya's migration management and border security efforts, stressing follow-up on the UN-facilitated Structured Dialogue outcomes.

### Egypt Reiterates Support for Libya's Unity in Talks with Malta (15–16 June)
Egyptian Foreign Minister Badr Abdelatty reaffirmed Cairo's support for Libya's unity, stability, and sovereignty, calling for the unification of state institutions.

### UK Lowers Travel Risk for Tripoli, Benghazi, Misrata (15 June)
The UK Foreign Office downgraded the travel risk rating for Tripoli, Benghazi, and Misrata from red (highest) to yellow, while maintaining a warning against non-essential travel to other areas.

### GNU Suspends Airport Visa-on-Arrival System (15 June)
The GNU temporarily suspended visa-on-arrival issuance at airports and air entry points to regulate entry procedures.

### GNU Bans Use of Private Firms for Official Mail
The GNU ordered public institutions to stop using private companies for official correspondence, restricting the service to the state postal provider.

### Members of Governance Track Voice Reservations Over Structured Dialogue Outcomes
Several members of the Governance Track within the UN-facilitated Structured Dialogue expressed reservations about both the process and its outcomes.
